The problem with {\ttfamily std\+::tuple} is that it is recursively implemented. That is fine when you have a small number of elements in your tuple, but as you increase the size of the tuple, you will pay a price in terms of compile time.

That is why reflect-\/cpp contains {\ttfamily \doxylink{classrfl_1_1_tuple}{rfl\+::\+Tuple}}, which in many ways behaves just like {\ttfamily std\+::tuple}, but it compiles considerably faster.

The only downside of {\ttfamily \doxylink{classrfl_1_1_tuple}{rfl\+::\+Tuple}} is that it cannot be used inside {\ttfamily constexpr} or {\ttfamily consteval} functions.

You can use the functions {\ttfamily \doxylink{namespacerfl_ac379420409251e5a3c0bea4a701daf0a}{rfl\+::get}}, {\ttfamily \doxylink{namespacerfl_ac71a9ec8b4d8fd347719083ec38c00f9}{rfl\+::make\+\_\+tuple}}, {\ttfamily \doxylink{namespacerfl_a8dc25580f934bb3178f2af9ed75ad045}{rfl\+::tuple\+\_\+element\+\_\+t}}, {\ttfamily \doxylink{namespacerfl_ae161c290da2ddf3d2ac1611c46bb6593}{rfl\+::tuple\+\_\+size\+\_\+v}}, {\ttfamily \doxylink{namespacerfl_a3b029acd3bf5327a91c529a5d6e1532e}{rfl\+::tuple\+\_\+cat}} or {\ttfamily \doxylink{namespacerfl_aaa94742afd7fde0d6d6cc59ae6d92989}{rfl\+::apply}} to create or access the tuple and they work the same way as their equivalents in the standard library. In fact, {\ttfamily \doxylink{namespacestd_aae41d8280b7ccc3f0e0a0be2dab21c8f}{std\+::get}} will also work on {\ttfamily \doxylink{classrfl_1_1_tuple}{rfl\+::\+Tuple}}.
