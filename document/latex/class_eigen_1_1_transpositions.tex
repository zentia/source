\doxysubsection{详细描述}
\subsubsection*{template$<$\mbox{\hyperlink{sugar_8h_ae4f82344f573c70aa1066a2394bba345}{int}} Size\+At\+Compile\+Time, \mbox{\hyperlink{sugar_8h_ae4f82344f573c70aa1066a2394bba345}{int}} Max\+Size\+At\+Compile\+Time, typename \+\_\+\+Storage\+Index$>$\newline
class Eigen\+::\+Transpositions$<$ Size\+At\+Compile\+Time, Max\+Size\+At\+Compile\+Time, \+\_\+\+Storage\+Index $>$}
Represents a sequence of transpositions (row/column interchange) 


\begin{DoxyTemplParams}{模板参数}
{\em Size\+At\+Compile\+Time} & the number of transpositions, or Dynamic \\
\hline
{\em Max\+Size\+At\+Compile\+Time} & the maximum number of transpositions, or Dynamic. This optional parameter defaults to Size\+At\+Compile\+Time. Most of the time, you should not have to specify it.\\
\hline
\end{DoxyTemplParams}
This class represents a permutation transformation as a sequence of {\itshape n} transpositions $[T_{n-1} \ldots T_{i} \ldots T_{0}]$. It is internally stored as a vector of integers {\ttfamily indices}. Each transposition $ T_{i} $ applied on the left of a matrix ( $ T_{i} M$) interchanges the rows {\ttfamily i} and {\ttfamily indices}\mbox{[}i\mbox{]} of the matrix {\ttfamily M}. \doxylink{bench__gemm_8cpp_addc86e8508f14411ec98f521c520f875}{A} transposition applied on the right (e.\+g., $ M T_{i}$) yields a column interchange.

Compared to the class \doxylink{class_eigen_1_1_permutation_matrix}{Permutation\+Matrix}, such a sequence of transpositions is what is computed during a decomposition with pivoting, and it is faster when applying the permutation in-\/place.

To apply a sequence of transpositions to a matrix, simply use the operator \texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*} as in the following example\+: 
\begin{DoxyCode}{0}
\DoxyCodeLine{\mbox{\hyperlink{class_eigen_1_1_transpositions_a4d2a9fea9847a606a26e9895aacbe800}{Transpositions}}\ tr;}
\DoxyCodeLine{MatrixXf\ \mbox{\hyperlink{_tutorial___advanced_initialization___comma_temporary_8cpp_a5d2ded7a4c8830608d7b218f5f27c0a0}{mat}};}
\DoxyCodeLine{\mbox{\hyperlink{_tutorial___advanced_initialization___comma_temporary_8cpp_a5d2ded7a4c8830608d7b218f5f27c0a0}{mat}}\ =\ tr\ *\ \mbox{\hyperlink{_tutorial___advanced_initialization___comma_temporary_8cpp_a5d2ded7a4c8830608d7b218f5f27c0a0}{mat}};}

\end{DoxyCode}
 In this example, we detect that the matrix appears on both side, and so the transpositions are applied in-\/place without any temporary or extra copy.
